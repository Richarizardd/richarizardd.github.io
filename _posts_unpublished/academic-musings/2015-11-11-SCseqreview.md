<h4 class="section-heading">Introduction</h4>
* It is still common practice to distinguish cell types based on a small set of marker genes i.e., fluorescence-activated cell sorting (FACS)

  * this method is inherently constrained, as a pre-selection of marker genes limits the resolution and variability of understanding a population of cells

  * Even cells of the same type can show substaintial gene expression variability leading to phenotypic variation
  
* A better approach is to analyze cells randomly drawn from a sample
  * Single cell analysis of a small number of genes
    * Single-molecule fluorescence in situ hybridization
    * Flow Cytometry using cell surface markers or fluorescent reporter proteins
